# Marvell Technology — Hyperfocused Schema

## Investment Mandate

Marvell is the second wave of AI silicon — the custom ASIC opportunity. As hyperscalers (Google, Amazon, Microsoft, Meta) build custom AI chips to reduce NVIDIA dependence and optimize for their specific workloads, Marvell co-designs and manufactures these chips. Marvell earns silicon revenue tied to production volume — recurring revenue that grows with hyperscaler AI deployment, not just CapEx.

**Core thesis:** Hyperscaler custom silicon is a $50B+ market by 2028. Marvell is the leading custom ASIC design partner. Every chip Google, Amazon, or Microsoft deploys at scale is a multi-year Marvell revenue stream.

## Standing Knowledge

### Custom ASIC Programs

- **Google:** TPU (training) and MPU (Memory Processing Unit, inference) — Marvell confirmed co-designer. ~2M units discussed. This is the largest known custom ASIC program.
- **Amazon:** Trainium (training) and Inferentia (inference) — Marvell co-designer. Trainium3 "Scorpio" ramp underway.
- **Microsoft:** Maia AI chip — Marvell involved in design.
- **Meta:** MTIA — Marvell involvement reported but less confirmed.

Each program is a 2–3 year design cycle followed by volume production. Revenue recognizes on silicon shipment (not design win). Programs typically extend across multiple generations.

### NVIDIA Strategic Investment

NVIDIA made a ~$2B strategic investment in Marvell (announced late March 2026). This is notable — NVDA investing in a company that co-designs alternatives to NVDA GPUs suggests NVIDIA sees custom ASIC as complementary (for inference scale-out) rather than purely competitive. It also validates Marvell's position in the ecosystem.

### The GAAP vs. Non-GAAP Gap

Marvell's GAAP earnings are suppressed by non-cash amortization of acquired intangibles (from Inphi 2021, Cavium 2018 acquisitions). Non-GAAP operating margin is already 35%. DCF model uses GAAP EBIT adjusted for R&D capitalization — this will show a deeply negative base year FCFF, with the model pricing almost entirely on terminal value. That is the correct mathematical result; the stock trades on long-run custom ASIC franchise, not current GAAP profits.

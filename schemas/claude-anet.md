# Arista Networks — Hyperfocused Schema

## Investment Mandate

Arista is the networking backbone of the AI data center. Every GPU cluster requires ultra-low-latency spine-leaf ethernet — Arista's specialty. As hyperscalers build larger clusters (10K → 100K+ GPUs), they need proportionally more 400G/800G ethernet switches. Arista's EOS software platform creates switching costs: once standardized across a hyperscaler's network, it is expensive and disruptive to replace.

**Core thesis:** AI cluster scale-out is a multi-year structural tailwind for high-speed ethernet. Arista is the market-defining supplier. EOS software creates recurring revenue and switching costs that extend the competitive moat beyond hardware.

---

## Standing Knowledge

### The EOS Moat

- **EOS (Extensible Operating System):** Arista's network OS runs on all their hardware. Hyperscalers standardize on EOS for their entire switching fabric — once embedded, replacing it means re-testing and reconfiguring the entire network. This is the same switching cost dynamic as Cisco IOS in enterprise, but Arista has won the AI data center market decisively.
- **CloudVision:** Management and analytics platform layered on EOS. Recurring SaaS-like revenue.
- **Gartner Magic Quadrant:** Arista is consistently positioned in the Leaders quadrant for Enterprise LAN and is rapidly gaining ground in data center networking.

### Port Speed Upgrade Cycle

AI clusters drive density and speed requirements that legacy networking cannot satisfy:
- **400G:** Current standard for AI spine-leaf. Arista dominant.
- **800G:** Next generation. Transition underway as 100K+ GPU clusters require higher throughput.
- **1.6T:** Following generation. Arista roadmap extending through 2027+.

Each upgrade cycle is a rip-and-replace for hyperscalers — new ASICs, new cabling, new software configuration. Arista earns revenue on each transition.

### AI Fabric Target

Management guided to AI fabric revenue of $3.5B in FY2026 (calendar year), representing direct hyperscaler network spend. This is a doubling from FY2025 and is the primary growth driver for the year.

### NVDA Competitive Overhang

NVIDIA announced it is entering the ethernet switching market (Spectrum-X platform). This is a risk: NVDA could leverage its hyperscaler relationships to sell integrated GPU+networking stacks. Near-term impact is minimal — Arista's installed base and EOS switching costs are strong. Monitor whether any hyperscaler announces a switch from Arista to NVDA Spectrum-X.

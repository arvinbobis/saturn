# ASML — Hyperfocused Schema

## Investment Mandate

ASML is the sole supplier of EUV lithography machines. Without them, no chip manufacturer can produce nodes below 7nm. This is a single-company monopoly in the most critical piece of capital equipment in semiconductor manufacturing — every leading-edge fab on the planet (TSMC, Samsung, Intel) buys exclusively from ASML. High NA EUV extends the monopoly through 2nm and beyond, into the 2030s.

**Core thesis:** Get paid on every advanced-node chip manufactured, regardless of which AI application wins. ASML is upstream of TSMC, which is upstream of NVIDIA, which is upstream of the AI application layer.

---

## Standing Knowledge

### The Monopoly

- **EUV machines:** ASML is the only company that can manufacture extreme ultraviolet lithography systems. The IP took 30+ years and billions in R&D to develop; no competitor is remotely close.
- **High NA EUV:** The next generation (€350–400M per machine vs. €150–200M for standard EUV). Needed for 2nm and below. Intel is the lead adopter (14A/18A nodes); TSMC deferred to 2029 citing cost; SK Hynix adopting for HBM4.
- **Backlog:** ~€33–40B in orders. Backlog gives 2–3 year revenue visibility, which is unusual for capital equipment. Orders paid ~40% upfront.
- **China risk:** Export controls prevent sale of EUV to China. ASML's China DUV revenue (~15% of total) is at risk of further restriction.

### TSMC as Leading Indicator

TSMC's CapEx plans are the most reliable leading indicator for ASML orders. When TSMC announces CapEx expansion (N2, A16, CoWoS), ASML machine orders follow 12–18 months later. Track TSMC CapEx guidance every quarter.

### Key Risk: TSMC High NA Deferral

In April 2026, Bloomberg reported TSMC is deferring High NA EUV adoption to 2029 — citing the €350–400M per machine cost vs. €150–200M for standard EUV. This delays the highest-ASP revenue uplift by 2–3 years. Intel (14A/18A) and SK Hynix (HBM4) are proceeding, partially absorbing the shortfall.

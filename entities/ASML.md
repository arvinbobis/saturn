# ASML Holding N.V. (ASML)

> The sole supplier of EUV lithography machines — without ASML, no one makes leading-edge chips.
> The most upstream chokepoint in the entire semiconductor supply chain.

## Overview

- **Business model:** Capital equipment manufacturer. Sells lithography machines (EUV and DUV) to chipmakers (TSMC, Samsung, Intel, SK Hynix). Revenue = machine shipments × ASP per machine + service/parts (~25–30% of revenue, high-margin, recurring).
- **Moat:** Monopoly on EUV. Duopoly (with Nikon) on immersion DUV. 30+ years of accumulated optics and light-source expertise cannot be replicated on any reasonable timeline.
- **Value chain position:** Upstream of TSMC. ASML order book is a 12–18 month leading indicator of TSMC and SK Hynix CapEx. No ASML EUV = no N3, N2, A16, or HBM leading-edge production.
- **Founded:** 1984, Veldhoven, Netherlands. HQ still in Veldhoven.
- **Listed:** NASDAQ: ASML / Euronext: ASML
- **Scale:** ~42,000+ employees.

### Why ASML Is in This Portfolio
Every leading-edge chip — TSM wafers, MU HBM, NVDA Blackwell — requires EUV lithography that only ASML makes. ASML participates in every node advancement automatically, regardless of which chipmaker wins. It earns on the buildout, not the outcome.

### EUV Machine Economics
- One EUV machine: ~€150–200M+
- High NA EUV (next generation, for A16/2nm): ~€350–400M
- Service contracts: 25–30% of revenue, ~50%+ gross margin
- Lead time for new machines: 12–18 months; TSMC orders 18–24 months ahead

## Moat

### EUV Monopoly
ASML is the only company in the world that manufactures EUV lithography machines. EUV uses 13.5nm wavelength light to print features at sub-5nm precision. The machine requires: Carl Zeiss (optics), Trumpf (CO₂ laser), Cymer (light source) — ASML owns or has exclusive relationships with all critical subsystems. 30+ years and €10B+ in cumulative R&D. No credible alternative supplier exists within the investment horizon.

### High NA EUV — Extending the Lead
High NA EUV (0.55 numerical aperture vs. current 0.33) enables A16/2nm and beyond. TSMC and Intel are the first customers. This extends ASML's monopoly another node generation, ~2026–2030.

### Installed Base and Service Revenue
Every ASML machine sold creates a multi-decade service relationship. Software updates, optical component replacement, field service — all from ASML. This recurring revenue is ~25–30% of total and grows with the installed base even when new machine shipments are lumpy.

### Export Controls as Competitive Protection
US/Dutch export controls prevent ASML from shipping EUV to China. This is a revenue headwind for ASML (China was ~15–20% of revenue) but also a regulatory moat — it blocks any Chinese competitor from accessing the same technology to eventually compete.

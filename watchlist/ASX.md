# ASE Technology Holding (NYSE: ASX)

*Scouted: 2026-05-30 | Status: Watching*

---

## Why Scouted

CNBC reported (May 13, 2026): "The next AI bottleneck will be in back-end packaging" — and ASE is the capacity gatekeeper. NVIDIA has pre-committed the majority of TSMC's in-house CoWoS capacity; ASE is the primary outsourced overflow — TSMC routes chiplet packaging steps to ASE and its subsidiary SPIL when CoWoS-L demand exceeds internal capacity. Jensen Huang personally attended the grand opening of a new ASE/SPIL site in Taiwan in 2025, signaling the relationship is strategic, not arm's-length.

## Chokepoint Thesis

| Filter | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Can't bypass?** | Yes — CoWoS and 2.5D/3D packaging requires specialized OSAT infrastructure; cannot be replicated quickly (capital and qualification time: 2–3 years minimum) |
| **Stack-agnostic?** | Yes — serves TSMC, Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron, Qualcomm, AMD; gets paid regardless of which GPU wins |
| **Defensible 5–10 years?** | Yes — $7B 2026 CapEx plan; ~30% OSAT global market share; qualification cycles create 18–24 month customer lock-in |

## Key Data Points

- **Market position:** World's largest OSAT (outsourced semiconductor assembly and test); ~30% global market share
- **2026 CapEx:** $7 billion — described as "record" and signals "new gatekeeper of AI" by financial media
- **Revenue:** Advanced packaging revenue forecast to reach $3.5B in 2026 (some estimates: doubling to $3.2B)
- **Pricing power:** Raising backend packaging prices 5–20% in 2026 on AI demand
- **AI services:** Chiplet assembly, 2.5D integration, SiP (system-in-package) for AI accelerators
- **Subsidiary:** SPIL (Siliconware Precision Industries) under same holding; SPIL new site opened 2025 (Jensen Huang attended)

## Key Risk

- **TSMC vertical integration:** TSMC is expanding in-house CoWoS capacity aggressively (127K WPM target). If TSMC fully internalizes advanced packaging, ASE's overflow role shrinks. Mitigation: chiplet heterogeneous integration requires multiple packaging architectures; ASE's cost structure for mature steps is more competitive than TSMC internal.
- **China competition:** JCET (JCY) and UTAC are Chinese OSAT competitors; US export controls limit their participation in leading-edge work, protecting ASE's advanced packaging position.
- **Commodity OSAT:** The bulk of ASE's revenue is still conventional packaging (lead frames, QFN) — lower margin and more commoditized than the advanced packaging segment.

## Status: Watching

Research session needed to quantify: (1) advanced packaging as % of total ASE revenue, (2) margin profile of OSAT vs. TSMC in-house CoWoS, (3) ASX P/E and DCF comparability to the SATURN portfolio.

*Source: CNBC (May 13, 2026), Tekne Capital CEO interview (CNBC, May 12), ASE company disclosures.*

# GEV — GE Vernova

**Status:** Watching
**Added:** 2026-05-28 (Session 30)
**Scouted via:** Phase 1c chokepoint scan

---

## Why Scouted

GE Vernova is the largest pure-play power generation and grid infrastructure company after its spin-off from GE (April 2024). In 2026, AI datacenter power demand has become the most acute physical constraint in the AI buildout — hyperscalers report they cannot build data centers fast enough because grid power is unavailable or utility connections are 3-5 years away. GEV makes the gas turbines, combined-cycle plants, and grid electrification equipment that serve as the gating constraint for new datacenter capacity.

---

## Choke Point Thesis

1. **Can't bypass it:** Every AI datacenter requires grid power. GEV turbines have 3-5 year lead times; the company is now sold out through 2028 and largely into 2030. No AI factory can operate without a power source — and GEV holds ~40% of the US gas turbine market.
2. **Stack-agnostic:** GEV powers all AI datacenter operators (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta) regardless of GPU vendor (NVIDIA, AMD) or custom ASIC platform. It is agnostic to every layer above the power meter.
3. **Defensible 5–10 years:** The $163B backlog (Q1 2026 record) provides 3-4 year revenue visibility; turbine service contracts run 20-30 years; US data center power demand is projected to rise 130% from 2024 to 2030 (Lawrence Berkeley National Lab / BloombergNEF). The barrier to entry is massive (nuclear/defense-level manufacturing, specialized metallurgy, specialized talent).

## Key Risk

- **Not semiconductor supply chain:** GEV is upstream of AI infrastructure but not in the semiconductor manufacturing supply chain — it is an industrial/energy company. Portfolio fit is looser than COHR, LRCX, or the core 7.
- **Valuation:** At ~$1,100 with Goldman at $1,328, margin of safety is thin if the AI DC power buildout slows or interconnects to grid happen faster via utilities.
- **Geopolitical:** US natural gas pricing, permitting risk for new peaker plants, policy risk if AI electricity demand triggers regulatory scrutiny.

# COHR — Coherent Corp

**Status:** Watching
**Added:** 2026-05-23 (Session 12)
**Scouted via:** Phase 1c chokepoint scan

---

## Why Scouted

Coherent Corp is the only vertically integrated photonics manufacturer operating 6-inch Indium Phosphide (InP) wafer fabs. InP is the substrate required for high-speed optical transceivers that interconnect GPUs, switches, and servers across AI data centers. No AI data center at 400G/800G/1.6T scale operates without these transceivers — they are as foundational to AI infrastructure as HBM or CoWoS packaging.

Additional signal: Coherent supplies the optical components (CO₂ laser system) inside ASML's EUV lithography equipment, placing it in two non-overlapping AI supply chains simultaneously.

---

## Choke Point Thesis

1. **Can't bypass it:** AI data centers at 1.6T speeds require optical transceivers; copper cables cannot match reach or power efficiency at datacenter scale; InP-based components are required for 400G, 800G, and co-packaged optics at 1.6T.
2. **Stack-agnostic:** COHR serves all hyperscalers (Amazon, Microsoft, Google, Meta) regardless of GPU vendor (NVIDIA, AMD, Google TPU, Amazon Trainium) and all switch vendors (ANET, MRVL, Broadcom).
3. **Defensible 5-10 years:** 6-inch InP wafer fabs are a capital-intensive, multi-year manufacturing moat. No announced competitor is building comparable 6-inch InP capacity. Silicon photonics is a long-run risk (5+ years from performance parity at key wavelengths).

---

## Key Risks

- **Silicon photonics threat:** Intel, TSMC, and Broadcom are developing silicon photonics alternatives; if Si-Ph reaches performance parity at key wavelengths, InP moat weakens.
- **Customer concentration:** Data center optics revenue concentrated in major hyperscalers; pricing pressure from customers seeking transceiver cost reduction.
- **Legacy business drag:** Coherent has industrial laser and defense optics businesses that dilute the AI margin profile and complicate the investment story.

## Choke Point Thesis

VAT Group (ticker: VACN on SIX Swiss Exchange) is the world's leading manufacturer of ultra-high vacuum valves and related equipment. Vacuum valves are essential components in every major semiconductor fab tool — etch, deposition (CVD/PVD/ALD), and inspection equipment cannot operate without precise vacuum isolation at sub-millibar pressures. Key facts:

- Estimated 30–40% market share in semiconductor vacuum valves globally
- Supplies ASML, Applied Materials (AMAT), Lam Research (LRCX), Tokyo Electron (TEL), and KLA directly — their tools cannot be delivered to TSMC, Samsung, or Intel without VAT valves
- Products are qualified into specific fab tool models; requalification takes 12–18 months
- Every CoWoS run at TSMC, every HBM stack at SK Hynix, and every EUV exposure at Intel passes through a fab that relies on VAT vacuum components
- Revenue CHF 1.074B in 2025 (+14% YoY, underlying +20%); semiconductor business unit +15%

**Passes all three thesis filters:**
1. ✅ Supplies something the AI buildout cannot proceed without — no fab tool operates without vacuum valves; TSMC's 140K WPM CoWoS target requires proportional vacuum components throughout
2. ✅ Stack-agnostic — supplies all chipmakers (TSMC, Samsung, Intel, Micron) and all tool vendors (AMAT, LRCX, TEL, ASML) regardless of which GPU architecture or AI stack wins
3. ✅ Defensible for 5-10 years — ultra-high vacuum valve manufacturing requires extreme precision and materials science; qualification cycles prevent rapid substitution; Swiss precision manufacturing heritage (Haag, Switzerland)

# LRCX — Lam Research Corporation

**Status:** Watching
**Added:** 2026-05-25 (Session 18)
**Scouted via:** Phase 1c chokepoint scan

---

## Why Scouted

Lam Research is the dominant supplier of high-aspect-ratio etch tools — specifically the equipment that creates Through-Silicon Vias (TSVs), the vertical copper interconnects that physically connect stacked DRAM dies in HBM. No HBM can be produced without TSV etching. As HBM transitions to HBM4 (16-Hi stacks with tighter pitch), the complexity and per-wafer etch time increase, making Lam's process capability increasingly critical. Lam reported HBM-related tool revenue +50% YoY in 2025–2026 and holds the dominant position in etch for advanced memory stacking.

Secondary angle: Lam also supplies deposition and etch tools for Gate-All-Around (GAA) transistor architecture at 2nm — now in commercial production at TSMC and Samsung — and is critical for advanced packaging processes including CoWoS.

---

## Choke Point Thesis

1. **Can't bypass it:** HBM production requires TSV etching at each stacking layer. As HBM moves from 12-Hi to 16-Hi stacks, the number of etch steps per HBM4 die increases. There is no TSV-free alternative for HBM stacking.
2. **Stack-agnostic:** Lam tools are used by all three HBM producers (SK Hynix, Samsung, Micron) regardless of which GPU architecture (NVDA, AMD, custom ASICs) eventually uses the finished HBM4.
3. **Defensible 5–10 years:** MODERATE-HIGH. Lam holds ~50%+ share in etch but faces competition from Tokyo Electron (TEL) and Applied Materials (AMAT) in some adjacent processes. The TSV etch specifically is Lam-dominated due to deep technical entrenchment. Equipment refresh cycles (3–5 years) and installed base service revenue provide recurring revenue moat.

---

## Key Risk

- Not a monopoly like ASML. TEL and AMAT are credible competitors in adjacent etch and deposition processes.
- Equipment companies are more cyclical than ASML (EUV is a singular product; Lam competes across multiple tool categories).
- Semiconductor equipment is subject to US export controls (relevant for any China exposure).
- Current market cap ~$90B; not cheap. Valuation expansion requires continued memory capex expansion.

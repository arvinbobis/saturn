# Qnity Electronics (NYSE: Q)

*Scouted: 2026-05-30 | Status: Watching*

---

## Why Scouted

CNBC highlighted "a low-profile stock in the semiconductor supply chain that has doubled this year" (May 13, 2026) — identified as Qnity Electronics, spun off from DuPont de Nemours in November 2025. Jim Cramer has also flagged it as "under-the-radar." Qnity provides specialty chemicals and advanced materials that are literally consumed in every wafer production cycle — consumables that cannot be substituted during a running process qualification.

## Chokepoint Thesis

| Filter | Assessment |
|--------|-----------|
| **Can't bypass?** | Yes — specialty chemicals (dielectrics, CMP slurries, photoresists for EUV, thermal interface materials) are required inputs at every advanced node; switching suppliers requires a new process qualification (6–18 months minimum per fab) |
| **Stack-agnostic?** | Yes — sells to TSMC, Samsung, SK Hynix, Micron, Intel; materials are architecture-neutral |
| **Defensible 5–10 years?** | Yes — DuPont's 100+ year materials science history; deep co-development relationships at each node transition; qualification lock-in creates sticky recurring revenue |

## Key Data Points

- **Origin:** Spun out of DuPont de Nemours, November 2025; NYSE: Q
- **Market cap:** ~$33B as of May 2026
- **2026 guidance:** Revenue $4.97–5.17B; adjusted EPS $3.55–3.95
- **Q1 2026:** Adjusted EPS $1.08 (beat $0.93 consensus); net sales $1.32B (+18% YoY)
- **Stock performance:** Up ~90% YTD 2026 from post-spin debut
- **Business focus:** Specialty chemicals for signal integrity, thermal management, and advanced packaging — all AI data center-relevant applications
- **Valuation:** Forward P/E ~32–38× (below peer specialty materials peers)

## Key Risk

- **Pure materials play, not systems:** Qnity does not design chips — it sells chemicals to chip makers. Revenue is volume × price, not design-win dependent. Pricing power depends on how "specialty" its formulations are vs. commoditized alternatives.
- **DuPont legacy overhang:** Post-spin execution risk — management team, treasury policy, and customer relationships all need to prove independence from DuPont corporate.
- **Concentration:** If materials are consumed predominantly at one node (e.g., CoWoS or EUV), a process architecture shift could reduce specific product demand.
- **Not semiconductor infrastructure per se:** Qnity is closer to a specialty chemicals company than a semiconductor company — lower gross margin profile than fabless chip designers; DCF will look different from NVDA/MRVL.

## Status: Watching

Research session needed to confirm: (1) exact product categories and which fabs/nodes they serve, (2) gross margin profile vs. semiconductor names already in portfolio, (3) whether the "specialty" moat is truly qualification-locked or substitutable with 12-month lead time.
